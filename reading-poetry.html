The point

Reading a poem draws on explicit skill (prosody, convention, allusion) and tacit, embodied skill (breath, stress, articulation) at once — and the two pull against each other: reading for meaning versus reading for stress.

The study Porter & Machery (2024). “AI-Generated Poetry Is Indistinguishable from Human-Written Poetry and Is Rated More Favorably,” Scientific Reports 14. doi.
  • 1,634 non-expert readers judged AI vs. human poems by ten canonical poets.
  • Correct only 46.6% of the time — below chance; more likely to call an AI poem “human” than a real one.
  • Blind, the AI poems were rated higher on rhythm and beauty — and that fluency was the tell readers misread as human.

The skill that catches the difference — hearing where a real poem’s rhythm resists you — is the tacit part, rarely taught. Scanning gives the ear something explicit to hold.
